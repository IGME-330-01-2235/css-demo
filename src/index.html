css-04 : Normal Flow

This branch demonstrates the Normal Flow of an HTML/CSS document.

It adds 40px of padding on the <html> element.

See how block-level elements like <h1>, <h2>, and <p> take up 100% of the width afforded to them? They also are rendered top-to-bottom, being provided with as much height as their content needs, and wrap wne necessary.

Block elements can have margin (spacing outside, between the border and neighboring elements) and padding (spacing inside, between the border and the content).

Inline elements (like this strong) can have left/right margin and padding, but no top/bottom margin (only padding, and even then, it doesn't affect neighbors).

Understanding how the CSS Box Model (margin, border, padding, content) and Normal Flow work, and building your page to take advantage of what it wants to do normally, is what I mean when I say "Stop Fighting CSS!"
